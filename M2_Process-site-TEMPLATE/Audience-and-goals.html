Audience and Goals

After conducting preliminary research, we determined who our audiences were and the goals applicable to those audiences.

Audience

To better understand and configure the direction of the website redesign, my first step was to begin by narrowing down the intended audience to be targeted specifically. To put this into action, I began by starting broad and gradually moving towards more and more specific groupings.

The initial step I used in this process was to define the perceived broad goals of my organization, which in turn served to form as a guideline of general direction for proceeding.

Next, I used the broad goals as a jumping point to further narrow the targeted audience into 6 categories: Skeptics, Audience (recipients), Audience (non-involved), Partners, Members, and Donors/Sponsors.

After having created the categories, the process continued, with the next step involving the assignment of people, groups, industries, or organizations into the various categories. This aspect was challenging due to many of the audience members being applicable to multiple categories, but the categorization decision ultimately came to be made by placing the various audiences with their assumed “best-fits”.

The last step to this part of the process entailed further refinement, which inevitably led to the decision to focus on 3 primary categories to target: Audience (recipients & advocates), Sponsors, and volunteers.

Having specified the intended audience for the organization, the next step would come to involve defining applicable goals.

Goals

Overarching Goals

Audience
  • Serve as an outlet to attract more donors and volunteers
  • Educate and bring awareness to lack of healthy grocery options for welfare recipients and local populations
  • Allow possible recipients to become introduced to the organization and benefit from its services
  • Spread the word about the organization and its cause
  • Function as a designated access point to information on and about the organization
Client
  • Acquisition of further monetary support
  • Expansion of organization in terms of members and volunteers
  • Increased level of outreach in terms of area and regions delivered to
  • Increase in events/conventions the organization is invited to
  • Further spread of awareness towards the organization and issue it is tackling
  • Better education of the local community on the issue
  • Provide contact information for farmers/growers to reach the organization.
Website
  • Easy user navigation on the site
  • Providence of concise but important impact of the organization
  • Easy access to educational information on the organization and its cause
  • Professional presentation of organization’s brand
  • Easy and understandable access to information on events and delivery dates

Specific Goals

User Goal Content/Action
Families with Young Children Provide information on distribution process and schedule, organization’s goals, importance of healthy eating, benefits, and how to get involved. Email sign-up, social media following, volunteer or join as a recipient, attend events, advocate on behalf of the organization.
Schools/Education Advocates Provide educational resources and opportunities/information on event hosting and organization’s schedule. Become an outlet for distributing educational material, advocate for cause, or host an informational event.
Retired Elderly Spread awareness, promote healthy eating, inform of organizational goals, explain scheduling, as well as means of distribution and contact. Host informational events at nursing homes, join cause as recipients, support and advocate on behalf of organization, join newsletter, follow blog.
Donors/Sponsors Introduce goals, benefits, information on getting involved and supporting the organization, and means of contact. Donate, sponsor programs, promote organization content, affiliate with cause, become a partner.
Volunteers Provide organization goals, explanation of process, and outlet for joining. Increase in volunteer numbers, associate with cause, promote services.
Farms/Independent Providers Provide benefits, primary information on process and impact, and outlet for contact. Join as partner, offer services or donations, host or sponsor events, or advocate on behalf of organization.

Conclusion and Take Aways

Having undergone the process, the concluded take-away was a better understanding of what the website would be trying to accomplish, as well as who would be targeted as the main audience.
